Cultivators of Boulder's third space culture — maintaining federation partnerships with venues and grounding digital coordination in physical reality.

## Purpose

The Third Space Circle cultivates relationships with Boulder's third spaces — cafes, coworking spaces, makerspaces, and gathering places where scenius happens. They negotiate federation partnerships, coordinate venue use for network events, and ensure the network remains rooted in physical presence.

## Composition & Participation

- **Open to:** All [[Participant|Participants]], [[Member|Members]], [[Steward|Stewards]], and authorized [[Agent|Agents]]
- **Minimum:** 2 active participants with established third space relationships
- **Lead:** Optional facilitator role, rotating, selected by circle members
- **Local Presence Required:** Circle work requires physical presence in Boulder bioregion

## Federation Agreement Elements

Third space federation agreements include:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Mutual Recognition** | Network recognizes venue as partner; venue recognizes network members |
| **Space Access** | Terms for network use of physical space |
| **Event Hosting** | Framework for hosting network events |
| **Knowledge Sharing** | How learnings flow between network and venue community |
| **Financial Terms** | If any — venue fees, revenue sharing, in-kind exchange |

## Event Cycle Responsibilities

| Phase | Circle Focus |
|-------|--------------|
| **Inter-Event** | Relationship cultivation, new partner outreach, community presence |
| **Pre-Event** | Venue booking, logistics coordination, partner briefing |
| **Event** | On-site coordination, partner liaison, space management |
| **Post-Event** | Partner feedback, relationship maintenance, agreement renewal |

## Agent Participation

[[Agent|Agents]] support the Third Space Circle through:
- Maintaining partner registry in knowledge graph
- Tracking agreement terms and renewals
- Coordinating scheduling across venues
- Flagging opportunities for new partnerships
